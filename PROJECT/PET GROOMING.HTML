Pet Care Tips

Nutrition Guidelines

Feed your pets high-quality food appropriate for their age, size, and breed. Avoid giving them human food that can be toxic.

Grooming Schedule

Regular grooming prevents matting, reduces shedding, and helps detect skin issues early. Brush your pet at least twice a week.

Exercise Needs

Daily exercise keeps pets healthy and prevents behavioral problems. Dogs need walks, while cats enjoy interactive play sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I groom my pet?

For most dogs, grooming every 4-6 weeks is ideal. Cats generally groom themselves but may need assistance with mat removal and nail trimming every 2-3 months.

What vaccines does my pet need?

Dogs typically need DHPP, rabies, and bordetella vaccines. Cats need FVRCP and rabies. Puppies and kittens require a series of vaccines starting at 6-8 weeks old.

How can I tell if my pet is sick?

Signs of illness include loss of appetite, lethargy, vomiting, diarrhea, coughing, difficulty breathing, or changes in behavior. Contact us immediately if you notice these symptoms.

Do you offer emergency services?

Yes, we provide emergency veterinary care during our business hours. For after-hours emergencies, we recommend contacting the nearest 24-hour animal hospital.
